## Key Takeaways

- Blue Team focuses on defensive cybersecurity.
- SOC analysts are an important part of the Blue Team.
- SOC Level 1 analysts usually handle initial alert triage.
- SOC Level 2 analysts perform deeper investigations.
- SOC Level 3 analysts handle advanced security cases.
- SOC Engineers help maintain and improve security tools.
- Incident Responders handle confirmed security incidents.
- Threat Intelligence Analysts provide context about attackers and threats.
- Digital Forensics Analysts investigate digital evidence.
- GRC teams focus on governance, risk, and compliance.
- SOC career growth requires both technical and soft skills.

## Defensive Learning

- Cybersecurity defense requires teamwork across multiple roles.
- SOC analysts help detect and investigate suspicious activity.
- Clear escalation paths improve incident handling.
- Threat intelligence helps SOC teams understand attacker behavior.
- Incident response teams help contain and recover from security incidents.
- Documentation is important for investigations and reporting.
- Blue Team roles support continuous improvement in security posture.
- SOC careers can lead to advanced roles such as Threat Hunter, Incident Responder, SOC Engineer, or Blue Team Lead.
